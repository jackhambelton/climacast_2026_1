About ClimaCast

Weather warnings should give you clarity when it matters most.

Our purpose

To make severe-weather information easier to understand, easier to trust and, above all, easier to act on.

Product note / 01

ClimaCast is a UK weather intelligence product built around one question: what should I do?

  1. 01

    Read the warning without decoding technical language.

  2. 02

    Understand the likely effect on your place and plans.

  3. 03

    See a practical next step while there is still time to take it.

What we are building

A calmer layer between official weather information and the everyday decisions it affects.

01 / Definition

Not another forecast. A decision product.

Forecasts describe the atmosphere. Warnings describe risk. ClimaCast is designed to connect that risk to the choices people actually have to make.

We structure severe-weather information around timing, location, likely disruption and useful action. The aim is not to make weather look more dramatic. It is to make the situation more legible.

It is not A wall of charts and percentages.
It is not A feed built to keep you scrolling.
It is A short route from warning to decision.

02 / The product

Four layers. One clear view.

Signal intake 01 / 04

Official sources, continuously watched.

ClimaCast follows weather warnings and supporting information from trusted official sources, keeping the product focused on evidence rather than speculation.

Yellow warning · Rain South East England

Heavy rain may affect the evening journey home.

The product moves beyond the warning headline to explain when disruption is most likely, which plans may be affected and what can usefully be done next.

Source modelOfficial-first
OutputPlain-language briefing
Design goalUseful in under five seconds

03 / Method

From a national warning to your practical next step.

Find the signal

INPUT / 01

Start with official warnings and verified supporting information. Separate the important change from the constant background noise of ordinary weather.

Explain the impact

CONTEXT / 02

Translate severity, timing and geography into the things people recognise: a slower commute, a disrupted journey, a vulnerable home or a plan that needs moving.

Make it local

PLACE / 03

Organise information around UK regions, with more detail where the warning area is smaller and the consequences are more specific.

End with action

OUTPUT / 04

Finish with a useful, proportionate next step. No manufactured urgency and no vague instruction to “stay weather aware” without explaining what that means.

04 / Principles

The product has rules. These are the important ones.

Clarity over volume

Say less. Make every line useful.

More information does not automatically produce a better decision. ClimaCast prioritises the few details that materially change what someone should do.

Evidence over drama

Serious when needed. Never sensational.

Weather risk deserves careful language. The product distinguishes what is known, what is likely and what remains uncertain.

Access over attention

Safety information is not a retention trick.

Core severe-weather information should remain straightforward to reach, including for people who do not want an account.

Calm over clutter

Design for the stressful moment.

The interface is intended for rushed mornings, disrupted journeys and uncertain plans—not for a leisurely tour through a dashboard.

The direction

Build a weather product people can rely on without having to study it.

ClimaCast is being shaped around the ordinary moments where weather becomes a real decision: whether to leave earlier, change a route, protect a home or move a plan. That is the product—and the standard it should be judged by.
