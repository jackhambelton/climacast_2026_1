Eligibility

4 questions
Note: the 2026.1 Public Beta is open to anyone with a ClimaCast account in one of the 13 tracked UK regions. No invite code is required for this phase.
Who can join?

Any ClimaCast account holder located in a tracked region can opt in from the Labs section of their account settings. An account is required because beta toggles and feedback are saved per user.

Do I need to be in a specific region?

All 13 tracked UK regions are included in 2026.1. Some features, such as Hyperlocal Flood Timing, are more refined in regions with denser flood-sensor coverage — this is shown in the feature description when you toggle it on.

Is there a cost to join?

No. ClimaCast Labs and the 2026.1 Public Beta are free, in line with ClimaCast's ad-free, free-at-point-of-use core alerts.

Can businesses or organisations join?

The 2026.1 Public Beta is currently aimed at individual account holders. If you're interested in organisational access, use the feedback form to let us know and we'll get in touch about future phases.

Joining the beta

4 questions

From the main ClimaCast app, go to Settings → Labs and switch on "2026.1 Public Beta". This unlocks the Labs feature list on this site and enables beta toggles in your account.

You can then turn individual beta features on or off independently — joining the beta itself does not switch every feature on automatically.

No. Your core severe weather alerts continue exactly as before. Beta features are additional layers shown alongside your existing alerts, not replacements for them.

Yes. Each feature listed under Beta Features has its own toggle. You can enable just the ones that are useful to you and leave the rest off.

The 2026.1 Public Beta is available wherever you normally access ClimaCast. Some features, such as Voice Briefing, are still in earlier testing and may not be available on every device yet — this is noted against each feature.

What's new in 2026.1

3 questions

2026.1 widens access from a closed group to all eligible account holders, and promotes Hyperlocal Flood Timing and Commute Impact Score from "Testing" to "Live" status based on feedback from earlier phases.

Multi-Day Risk Outlook and Household Alert Sharing remain in "Testing" and are being rolled out gradually across regions.

The complete, current list — including status (Live, Testing, or Coming Soon) — is maintained on the Beta Features page and reflects what's available in 2026.1.

Future versions (e.g. 2026.2) will get their own page like this one. The Roadmap gives a general view of what's planned ahead of a formal release page.

Known issues

Tracked for 2026.1

These are issues we're already aware of in the current beta build. If you run into something not listed here, please use the feedback form so we can investigate.

Feature Issue Severity
Multi-Day Risk Outlook Outlook occasionally flags a "likely disruption" day one day later than the day it actually affects. We're tuning the timing model. Moderate
Household Alert Sharing Shared "leave by" times may not update instantly for other household members; a manual refresh of the alert feed resolves this. Minor
Commute Impact Score Score can briefly show as "Calculating" for newly added routes until the next scheduled data refresh, typically within 15 minutes. Minor
Hyperlocal Flood Timing Coverage in rural areas with fewer flood sensors may show wider time ranges than urban areas. This reflects current data density, not a bug. Minor
Voice Briefing Not yet available — listed here for visibility ahead of its planned rollout in a later phase. Minor

Feedback & data

2 questions

Use the feedback form on the Labs page, or the in-app prompt shown after a beta feature is used during a live weather event. Include the feature name and your region so we can investigate faster.

Enabling a beta feature may collect additional usage data specific to that feature (for example, whether a flood timing estimate matched what happened) so we can refine accuracy. This is covered in our Privacy Policy and only applies to features you've actively switched on.

Leaving the beta

2 questions

Yes. Turn off individual features from their toggles, or switch off "2026.1 Public Beta" entirely in Settings → Labs to return to the standard ClimaCast experience.

Your core alert settings and history are unaffected. Feedback you've already submitted helps us improve the features for everyone and isn't deleted, but no further beta-specific usage data is collected once you've opted out.
