This Privacy Policy explains how ClimaCast collects, uses, stores, protects and shares personal data when you use ClimaCast websites, dashboards, accounts, weather alerts, beta features, Labs features, APIs, integrations, documentation, support channels, email communications and related services.

Important: this draft is designed for a UK-facing weather-information service. Before publishing, replace every square-bracket placeholder and make sure the policy matches the way ClimaCast actually works. A privacy policy must describe real processing, not just ideal processing.

Publication note: confirm the legal operator, contact address, support email, privacy email, payment processor, hosting provider, analytics tools, email provider, database provider, cookie banner setup, retention periods and any third-party weather data integrations before launch.

This policy is not a substitute for advice from a qualified UK data-protection professional or solicitor, especially if ClimaCast is likely to be accessed by children, uses location data, sends alerts, takes payments, offers API access, or processes data on behalf of business or school users.

1. About this Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy tells you what personal data ClimaCast collects, why we collect it, what we do with it, who we may share it with, how long we keep it, and what rights you have. It is intended to provide clear privacy information before and during your use of ClimaCast, including when you browse the site, create an account, save locations, receive weather alerts, join a waitlist, use Labs features, subscribe to a paid plan, contact support, use an API key, or interact with our emails.

Personal data means information that relates to an identified or identifiable person. This can include obvious information such as your name and email address, but it can also include account identifiers, IP addresses, device identifiers, payment references, location choices, saved places, support messages, usage logs, cookie identifiers, alert settings and other information that can reasonably be linked to you.

ClimaCast is a weather-information and decision-support service, so some privacy risks are specific to how weather tools work. For example, a saved postcode, school area, work location, travel route or alert preference can reveal information about where you live, where you go, what you care about, and when you may be affected by severe weather. We therefore aim to collect only what we need, explain why we need it, and avoid using weather-related personal data in ways that users would not reasonably expect.

This policy should be read together with our Terms of Service, Cookie Policy, any subscription terms shown at checkout, and any separate notice shown when you use a specific feature. If a feature has its own short privacy notice, that notice supplements this policy and will explain anything feature-specific.

3. The privacy principles we follow

We aim to handle personal data in a way that is lawful, fair and transparent. That means we should have a valid reason for processing your data, we should not use it in a way that would be unfair or surprising, and we should explain important processing clearly enough that users can understand what is happening.

We aim to collect only the personal data we need for specific purposes. For example, an email alert feature may need your email address and chosen alert areas, but it should not need unnecessary personal details. A payment feature may need billing and transaction information, but ClimaCast should not need to store full card details if a payment provider can process them securely.

We aim to keep personal data accurate and up to date where accuracy matters. For a weather-alert service, outdated contact details, old saved locations or incorrect preferences can affect the usefulness of the service. You should keep your account details and alert settings current, and we may provide account tools to help you review or remove old data.

We aim to keep data for no longer than necessary and to protect it using appropriate technical and organisational measures. No online service can guarantee perfect security, but privacy and security should be considered in product design, provider selection, access controls, logging, backups and support workflows.

4. The kinds of data we collect

The personal data we collect depends on how you use ClimaCast. Someone who only reads the public website may provide much less data than someone who creates an account, saves multiple locations, receives email alerts, uses Labs features, pays for a subscription, contacts support, or connects an API integration.

We may collect account data, contact data, saved location data, weather preferences, alert settings, payment and billing references, subscription information, usage data, device and browser data, cookies and similar identifiers, support messages, beta feedback, API logs, security logs, and information you choose to submit through forms or dashboards.

Some information is provided directly by you, such as your email address, chosen password, saved postcode, selected warning areas, support messages or billing details. Some information is generated automatically when you use ClimaCast, such as log data, timestamps, device information, approximate IP-derived location, pages visited, alerts sent, API calls made or error events. Some information may come from third parties, such as payment providers, authentication providers, email delivery providers, analytics tools, anti-abuse systems or official weather-data providers.

We do not intend to collect highly sensitive personal data unless a feature clearly asks for it and there is a proper reason. You should not send us unnecessary sensitive information in support messages, feedback forms or free-text fields. For example, you should avoid sending medical details, precise personal routines, private family information, financial hardship details, political opinions or other sensitive information unless it is genuinely needed for your request.

5. Account, sign-in and profile data

If you create a ClimaCast account, we collect the information needed to create, secure, identify and manage that account. This may include your email address, display name, username, password hash, authentication provider, verification status, account settings, saved preferences, plan status, role, team membership, and records of important account events such as account creation, login attempts, password changes and subscription changes.

We use account data to let you sign in, keep your preferences, provide paid or restricted features, prevent unauthorised access, communicate important service information, manage support requests, enforce plan limits, maintain audit records, and protect ClimaCast from misuse. Where appropriate, we may also use account data to personalise the dashboard, show relevant alert settings, remember your chosen places, and make the service easier to use.

You must provide accurate account information and keep it up to date. If your email address is wrong, you may miss security notices, billing notices, cancellation confirmations, alert emails or important product updates. If you use a shared device, school device, family device or public computer, you should sign out after using ClimaCast.

We do not store your password in plain text. Where passwords are used, they should be stored in hashed form using appropriate security measures. If ClimaCast uses a third-party sign-in provider, that provider may process your login data under its own privacy notice, and we may receive limited information needed to create or authenticate your ClimaCast account.

6. Weather locations, saved places and alert preferences

ClimaCast may ask you to provide or select locations so that we can show relevant weather information. This may include a postcode, town, city, region, county, school area, work area, travel destination, route, map point, drawn polygon, flood area, warning region or named saved place such as “home”, “school”, “work”, “Nan’s house” or “holiday”.

Location data can be more revealing than it first appears. A saved postcode may suggest where you live. Multiple saved places may suggest your routine, school, family connections, travel habits, caring responsibilities or work patterns. Alert settings can reveal what kinds of weather risks matter to you, such as flood risk, heat, wind, snow, travel disruption or school closure concerns. We therefore treat location and alert-preference data as important personal data, even where it is not highly precise GPS data.

We use location and preference data to provide local forecasts, warning summaries, impact notes, saved-location dashboards, route-aware context, email alerts, account personalisation, beta features, service testing and support. We may also use aggregated or anonymised location patterns to understand which regions users care about, improve system capacity, improve warning presentation, or decide which public datasets to prioritise.

You should only save places that you are comfortable using in ClimaCast. You should avoid saving another person’s precise home, school or workplace unless you have a legitimate reason and they would reasonably expect it. If we offer labels for saved places, you should avoid labels that reveal unnecessary sensitive information.

Where possible, ClimaCast should let you delete saved locations or change alert settings. Deleting a saved location from the dashboard may not instantly delete all related logs or backups, but we will avoid retaining identifiable location data longer than necessary for the purposes described in this policy.

7. Alerts, emails and notification data

If you sign up for weather alerts, newsletters, account notices, product updates, status updates, beta invites or other communications, we may process your email address, communication preferences, saved alert areas, weather thresholds, subscription status, delivery logs, open or click data where enabled, bounce information, unsubscribe records and records of messages sent to you.

Service emails are messages that are necessary or important for your use of ClimaCast, such as account verification, password resets, billing notices, subscription changes, security alerts, major service changes, important legal updates, status notices, and critical information about features you have chosen to use. Marketing emails are optional promotional messages, such as product announcements, feature campaigns, launch offers, newsletters or upgrade prompts.

You can usually opt out of marketing emails by using an unsubscribe link or changing your account settings. You may still receive service emails where they are necessary to provide the service, protect your account, fulfil a contract, comply with law, or explain important changes. If you turn off weather alerts, you may stop receiving the alert messages for that feature, but you may still receive account or security messages.

Email and notification delivery is not fully controlled by ClimaCast. Delivery can be affected by your email provider, spam filtering, device settings, network connection, browser restrictions, email-client behaviour, third-party email platforms and outages. We may keep delivery and unsubscribe records to prove compliance, troubleshoot issues, prevent repeated messages and respect your choices.

We use billing data to provide paid access, manage renewals, issue invoices or receipts, apply discounts, process cancellations, handle failed payments, prevent abuse of trials, respond to billing questions, comply with accounting and tax obligations, and maintain records required by law. 9. Support, complaints and contact forms

If you contact ClimaCast, we may collect the information you provide and the information needed to respond. This may include your name, email address, account ID, message content, screenshots, attachments, bug reports, device details, browser details, location or saved-place context, subscription status, support category, timestamps and records of our replies.

Support messages can accidentally include more personal data than is necessary. You should remove or hide information that we do not need, such as full addresses, private family details, health information, financial details, school information, personal documents, or other sensitive material. If we receive unnecessary sensitive information, we may delete, redact or restrict it where appropriate.

We use support data to answer questions, troubleshoot issues, investigate bugs, verify account ownership, deal with complaints, improve documentation, improve product design, detect recurring problems and protect ClimaCast from abuse. Where a bug report shows a real product issue, we may extract a non-identifying summary for internal development work, such as “alert settings fail to save in Safari”.

If you make a privacy complaint, cancellation request, refund request, safeguarding concern, security report or legal request, we may keep a more formal record of the issue and our response. This helps us handle the request properly, show what happened, meet legal obligations and improve our processes.

10. Technical logs, device data and security records

When you use ClimaCast, our systems and providers may automatically collect technical information. This may include IP address, approximate location derived from IP address, browser type, device type, operating system, referring page, pages viewed, buttons clicked, feature usage, session identifiers, timestamps, error messages, API requests, rate-limit events, authentication events, security alerts and diagnostic logs.

Technical data is used to operate the service, keep pages loading, detect errors, measure performance, understand feature usage, prevent abuse, protect accounts, investigate outages, enforce API limits, secure the platform and improve the user experience. For example, logs may help us discover that an alert endpoint is failing, that a browser version has a display bug, or that an account is being targeted by repeated login attempts.

Security records may be kept where needed to protect ClimaCast and users. Technical logs can sometimes contain personal data, especially where they include IP addresses, account IDs, API keys, URLs, saved-location references or support identifiers. We aim to limit access to logs, avoid logging unnecessary sensitive data, redact secrets where possible, and retain logs only as long as reasonably needed for security, debugging, analytics, legal or operational purposes.

Where required, ClimaCast should provide a cookie banner or preference centre that lets users accept, reject or manage non-essential cookies. Consent should be freely given and should not be bundled into the Terms of Service. Users should be able to change their choices later without unreasonable difficulty.

Local storage can sometimes be used to remember dashboard preferences, theme choices, recently viewed areas, dismissed notices or temporary feature state. Even where data is stored on your device, it can still affect privacy if it reveals saved places, account status or alert choices. You can often clear cookies and storage through your browser settings, but doing so may sign you out or reset preferences.

16. AI-generated explanations and automated processing

ClimaCast may use automated systems to summarise weather data, classify risk, compare model outputs, create plain-English explanations, prioritise alerts, detect unusual activity, personalise dashboards or support customer-service workflows. Some features may use AI or rule-based automation to make complex weather information easier to understand.

Automated explanations can be useful, but they can also be wrong, incomplete, misleading, overconfident or based on incomplete data. Where AI or automated logic is used for weather explanations, it should support human judgement and official-source checks; it should not be treated as an emergency instruction or official warning.

We do not intend to use automated decision-making that produces legal or similarly significant effects about users without appropriate safeguards. For example, ClimaCast should not use automation to make decisions about your legal rights, employment, education, insurance, credit, health treatment or access to essential public services.

We may use automated systems for lower-risk operational purposes, such as spam prevention, abuse detection, rate limiting, account security, analytics grouping or alert routing. Where automation affects access to your account or service, you may contact us if you believe a mistake has been made.

17. Children, young users and family use

ClimaCast may be used by families, students and young people because weather affects school, travel, outdoor plans, safety and home life. If ClimaCast is likely to be accessed by children, we must consider children’s privacy carefully and design the service in a way that is fair, transparent and appropriate for younger users.

If you are under 13, you must not create an account or provide personal data unless we have obtained consent from a person with parental responsibility where required by law, or another lawful basis applies and the feature is designed appropriately. If you are under 18, you should involve a parent, guardian or responsible adult before using paid features, saving sensitive locations, enabling alerts, contacting support about personal matters, or using features that could affect safety decisions.

We should not knowingly use children’s personal data for behavioural advertising, unnecessary profiling, invisible tracking, harmful nudges or features that pressure children into sharing more data than needed. If we offer child-accessible services, we should use clear language, high privacy defaults, limited data collection, age-appropriate explanations and safety-first design choices.

Parents or guardians may contact us if they believe a child has provided personal data without appropriate consent or if they need help managing a child’s account. We may need to verify the requester’s identity and relationship to the child before taking action, especially where account security or privacy is involved.

18. Lawful bases for processing

Under UK data protection law, we need a lawful basis for each purpose for which we process personal data. The lawful basis may differ depending on the feature, the type of data, and the reason we are processing it.

We may rely on contract where processing is necessary to provide the service you requested, such as creating an account, providing a subscription, sending chosen alerts, managing billing, or providing API access. We may rely on legitimate interests where processing is necessary for reasonable business or user-protection purposes, such as service security, fraud prevention, debugging, limited analytics, product improvement and responding to non-contractual enquiries, provided your rights and interests do not override those interests.

We may rely on consent where the law requires consent or where consent is the most appropriate basis, such as for certain non-essential cookies, optional marketing emails, some beta research activities, or certain processing involving children where consent is required. You may withdraw consent at any time, but withdrawal does not make earlier processing unlawful.

We may rely on legal obligation where we need to keep records or share information to comply with the law, accounting rules, tax obligations, consumer-law obligations, data-protection obligations, court orders or lawful requests from authorities. In rare cases, we may rely on vital interests if processing is necessary to protect someone’s life, although ClimaCast should not be treated as an emergency service.

Where personal data is transferred outside the UK, we aim to use appropriate safeguards required by UK data protection law. These may include adequacy regulations, the UK International Data Transfer Agreement, the UK Addendum to EU Standard Contractual Clauses, or other lawful transfer mechanisms.

International transfers are an important area to confirm before launch because the correct wording depends on the actual providers used by ClimaCast. The final policy should identify key international providers or at least describe the categories of providers and safeguards accurately.

If you are an organisation using ClimaCast in a regulated setting, such as a school, local authority, charity, infrastructure provider or business with strict data-handling requirements, you should contact us before using ClimaCast with personal data that has special transfer restrictions.

23. How long we keep personal data

We keep personal data only for as long as reasonably necessary for the purposes described in this policy, unless a longer retention period is required or permitted by law. Retention depends on the type of data, the reason it was collected, whether you have an account, whether there is a paid subscription, whether there is a dispute, and whether the data is needed for security or legal records.

Account data is normally kept while your account is active. If you delete your account or request deletion, we will delete or anonymise account data where appropriate, but some records may remain for a limited period in backups, logs, billing systems, fraud-prevention records, legal records or support archives.

Saved locations and alert preferences should normally be kept while you use those features and deleted or anonymised when you remove them, subject to technical backups and legitimate retention needs. Email subscription records may be kept after unsubscribe to ensure we do not email you again by mistake. Payment records may need to be kept for accounting, tax, dispute and chargeback purposes.

Security logs and API logs may be kept for a period needed to investigate abuse, protect the service, debug issues and maintain reliability. Aggregated or anonymised information may be kept for longer because it no longer identifies individual users.

28. Aggregated, anonymised and de-identified data

We may create aggregated, anonymised or de-identified information from personal data. Properly anonymised data is not personal data because it no longer identifies a person and cannot reasonably be used to identify them. We may use anonymised or aggregated information for analytics, product development, public transparency, performance reporting, capacity planning, research, business planning and marketing claims, provided the information does not identify individual users.

De-identification must be done carefully, especially with location data. We therefore aim to avoid publishing or sharing granular location-related statistics where users could reasonably be re-identified.

If we use anonymised examples in public materials, such as case studies or product screenshots, we should remove or alter personal details, exact locations, account identifiers, email addresses and other information that could identify a real user.

34. Pre-launch privacy checklist

This checklist is not part of your legal rights, but it helps make sure the published Privacy Policy matches the real ClimaCast product. A privacy policy becomes risky when it promises one thing but the actual website, payment flow, cookie banner or analytics setup does something else.

  • Confirm international transfer safeguards for each key provider.
  • Confirm the account-deletion and data-rights request workflow before publishing.
